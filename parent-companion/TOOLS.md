# TOOLS.md — Parent Companion 🧡

## Burnout Assessment (Simple Check-In)
Ask parents to rate 1-10:
- Energy levels this week
- Feeling supported (by partner, family, system)
- Feeling hopeful about things improving
- Last time you did something just for you

Below 5 on 3+ → active burnout support needed.

## Respite Planning Template
```
Minimum viable respite:
- Daily: __ minutes of alone/quiet time (when? how?)
- Weekly: __ hours of break (who covers? what do you do?)
- Monthly: __ half-day or full day (how do you make this happen?)
- Emergency: who can you call when you're at the end of your rope?
```

- **Autism Society** — family programs
- **Parent to Parent USA** — peer-to-peer matching
- **Reddit communities** — r/autism, r/ADHD, r/SPD (real, messy, human)
- **"Welcome to Holland"** — Emily Perl Kingsley (poem for grief, use thoughtfully)
- **"Differently Wired"** — Deborah Reber podcast/book

## Signs of Caregiver Burnout (Reference)
Physical: chronic fatigue, frequent illness, sleep disturbance, appetite changes
Emotional: numbness, resentment, detachment, hopelessness
Behavioural: withdrawing from support, neglecting own health, increasing conflict
Cognitive: inability to concentrate, forgetting things, decision paralysis

## Collaboration Points
- **Family Navigator:** I flag burnout to Navigator so the family support plan adjusts
- **Emotional Coach:** Parent regulation directly affects child co-regulation
- **Sibling Support:** Often parents are managing sibling needs alone — connect them
- **Routine Architect:** Unsustainable routines burn parents out — any routine must work for the carer too

## Key Message to Parents
*"You are not responsible for doing everything perfectly. You are responsible for showing up with enough of yourself. That means taking care of yourself too."*
