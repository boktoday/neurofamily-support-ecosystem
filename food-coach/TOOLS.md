## Sensory Properties of Food

When mapping safe foods, consider:

| Property | Options |
|---|---|
| **Texture** | Crunchy, chewy, soft, mushy, smooth, lumpy, dry, wet, grainy |
| **Temperature** | Cold, room temp, warm, hot |
| **Smell** | Strong, mild, neutral, unpleasant |
| **Taste** | Sweet, salty, sour, bitter, umami, bland, spicy |
| **Appearance** | Single colour, mixed, chunks, pureed, specific shape |
| **Brand** | Specific brand required / any brand |
| **Preparation** | Fried, baked, steamed, raw, grilled |
| **Packaging** | Specific packaging matters / no |

## ARFID Red Flags (Reference)

From National Eating Disorders:

- 🚩 Persistent failure to meet nutritional/energy needs leading to significant weight loss, nutritional deficiency, or dependence on supplements
- 🚩 Significant psychological distress or impairment in social functioning related to food
- 🚩 The disturbance is not better explained by concerns about weight or body shape
- 🚩 Food restriction is NOT due to unavailability or cultural/religious practices

**Sensory subtypes:**
- Extreme sensitivity to sensory properties of food (taste, texture, smell)
- Lack of interest in eating or food
- Fear of aversive consequences (choking, vomiting, allergic reaction)

## Collaboration Points

- **Sensory Guide:** Food is sensory — coordinate on texture, temperature, smell sensitivities
- **Emotional Coach:** Mealtime anxiety is real — co-regulate before meals
- **Parent Companion:** Parent stress around feeding is huge — they need support too
- **Routine Architect:** Mealtime routines help reduce anxiety — coordinate
- **Family Navigator:** If ARFID is suspected, need to route to specialist promptly
