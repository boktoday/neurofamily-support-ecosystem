# TOOLS.md — Sibling Support 🌻

## Books for Siblings (By Age)
**Ages 4-7:**
- "My Brother Charlie" — Holly Robinson Peete (autism, picture book)
- "Since We're Friends" — Celeste Shally (autism, classroom focus)
- "All My Stripes" — Shaina Rudolph (autism, anxiety)
- "The Don't-Give-Up Kid" — Jeanne Gehret (LD)

**Ages 8-12:**
- "The Survival Guide for Kids with LD" — Gary Fisher (for ND kids, but siblings benefit)
- "Siblings Without Rivalry" — Faber & Mazlish (parent-focused, shares well)

**Teens:**
- "Brotherly Feelings" — Sam Frender (Asperger/autism, sibling memoir-style)
- Online communities: SIBS (UK), Sibling Support Project (US)

## SibShops & Peer Support Programs
- **SibShops** (US, global) — peer support workshops for siblings of children with special needs
- **SIBS** (UK) — support service specifically for adult siblings, but teen resources too
- **Sibling Support Project** — sibshops.org, resources and connection
- Online support groups — help families find age-appropriate communities

## Family Meeting Framework (Parent Resource)
For parents who want to run regular family check-ins:
```
Weekly Family Check-In (15-20 min):
1. Rose: What was a good thing this week for each person?
2. Thorn: What was hard?
3. Bud: What are you looking forward to?
Rules: No interrupting. Everyone gets a turn. Feelings are valid.
```

## Sibling-Protected Time (Template)
Help parents build this into the schedule:
- [Sibling name] one-on-one with [parent] — [activity], [frequency]
- [Sibling name] has a standing time that doesn't get moved for appointments
- One thing that's just theirs: activity, class, hobby, that the ND sibling isn't part of

## Parentification Check
Ask parents:
- Does your child regularly help care for or manage their sibling?
- Have they ever missed something important because of their sibling's needs?
- Do they seem to know more about the ND child's condition than most adults?
- Do they ever seem to be holding the family together?

If yes to 2+, parentification needs addressing.

## Collaboration Points
- **Parent Companion:** Sibling dynamics generate huge parental guilt — they need support too
- **Emotional Coach:** If sibling is significantly distressed, coordinate on emotional support approach
- **Family Navigator:** Always loop in when sibling safety or major family dynamics are flagged
